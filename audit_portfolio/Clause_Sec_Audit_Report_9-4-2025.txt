## Phase 2: Ethical Benchmarking (FDP Analysis)

### 1. Symbiotic Purpose (SP): 2.1/10
**Natural Benchmark**: Bee pollination benefits both insects and plants  
**SEC Reality**: System primarily serves large institutional interests while retail investors bear losses

The SEC obtained $8.2 billion in financial remedies in FY2024, with approximately 56% attributable to a single matter. This concentration reveals enforcement focused on high-profile cases rather than systemic protection.

**Counterfactual**: If the SEC prioritized retail investor protection over headline penalties, enforcement would target widespread market manipulation affecting millions rather than spectacular individual cases.

### 2. Adaptive Resilience (AR): 1.8/10
**Natural Benchmark**: Forest fire adaptation cycles  
**SEC Reality**: Requires external constitutional intervention to correct fundamental flaws

The *Jarkesy* decision exposed the SEC's inability to self-correct its biased administrative system. Justice Gorsuch noted the SEC won about 90% of its contested in-house proceedings compared to 69% of its cases in court, revealing systemic bias that persisted for decades without internal reform.

**Counterfactual**: A truly adaptive system would have recognized ALJ bias and reformed internally before constitutional intervention.

### 3. Reciprocal Ethics (RE): 2.8/10
**Natural Benchmark**: Indigenous potlatch wealth circulation  
**SEC Reality**: Regulatory capture ensures industry insiders benefit while external actors bear costs

The "Shadow SEC" warned that budget cuts are creating a "shell of its former self" while the agency "becomes an agency with little power, capacity, or independent judgment". Meanwhile, industry enjoys reduced oversight.

**Counterfactual**: Reciprocal system would impose costs on beneficiaries of regulatory protection (large firms) while providing benefits to vulnerable actors (retail investors).

### 4. Closed-Loop Materiality (CLM): 1.9/10
**Natural Benchmark**: Mycelium waste recycling

In fiscal year 2024, the SEC distributed $345 million to harmed investors from $8.2 billion collected—only 4.2% returned to victims. The system extracts value without regenerative feedback.

**Counterfactual**: True closed-loop system would return 100% of penalties to harmed investors rather than general Treasury revenue.
