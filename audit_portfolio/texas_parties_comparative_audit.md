## Phase 1: Structural Dissection (7ES Framework)

### INPUTS COMPARISON

#### Texas Democratic Party
- **Financial**: $1,134,677.95 spent in H1 2025
- **Human**: Actively hiring (Development Director position open)
- **Organizational**: New infrastructure fund (Agave Democratic Infrastructure Fund) focusing on training and recruitment
- **Ideological**: Progressive platform supporting education funding, abortion access, cannabis legalization, LGBT rights

**Weakness**: Chronic underfunding relative to governing party requirements

#### Republican Party of Texas
- **Financial**: $1,040,527.06 spent in H1 2025
- **Human**: Chaired by Abraham George, succeeded Matt Rinaldi in 2024
- **Institutional**: Controls all statewide offices, both legislative houses, majority congressional seats
- **Organizational**: Recent 2024 convention amid "depleted finances and spirited infighting"

**Weakness**: Internal fragmentation and financial depletion despite institutional control

### PROCESSING COMPARISON

#### Texas Democratic Party
- **Centralized**: Austin headquarters with distributed organizing
- **Adaptive**: "Strength depends on how many Texas Democrats are talking to their neighbors, empowering their communities"
- **Innovation**: New PAC focuses on "training campaign staff, recruiting down-ballot candidates and gauging public opinion"

#### Republican Party of Texas
- **Hierarchical**: Austin headquarters, legally considered a political action committee
- **Platform-driven**: Formal platform development process through convention
- **Institutional**: Leverages existing governmental apparatus

### CONTROLS & FEEDBACK

#### Texas Democratic Party
- **Grassroots feedback**: Community organizing emphasis
- **Adaptive controls**: Responsive to coalition needs
- **Limited enforcement**: Minority party status reduces disciplinary capacity

#### Republican Party of Texas
- **Institutional controls**: Governmental power reinforcement
- **Platform discipline**: Formal platform enforcement through legislative action
- **Strong enforcement**: Majority party apparatus
