# **Texas Constitution Audit Report**  
**System Type:** Governance/Legal Framework  
**Audit Framework:** 7ES, FDP, DQD, OCF

## **1. 7ES Structural Dissection**  
| **Element**       | **Analysis** | **Weaknesses** |  
|-------------------|-------------|----------------|  
| **Input**         | Amendments, court rulings, legislative acts | Rigid amendment process (requires voter approval) |  
| **Output**        | Laws, regulations, judicial precedents | Overly complex (500+ amendments; longest state constitution) |  
| **Processing**    | Legislative sessions, judicial review | Slow adaptation (e.g., school finance lawsuits persist for decades) |  
| **Controls**      | Separation of powers, checks and balances | Weak executive constraints (Governor’s emergency powers) |  
| **Feedback**      | Voter referendums, court challenges | Delayed (e.g., 2023 property tax cuts took 5 years to pass) |  
| **Interface**     | Citizens, legislators, courts | Opaque (e.g., ballot language obscures amendment impacts) |  
| **Environment**   | Political polarization, population growth | Mismatched with modern needs (e.g., outdated infrastructure clauses) |  

**Key Weakness:**  
- **Brittle Processing** (requires supermajority for reforms).  
- **Opaque Interface** (voters lack clear information on amendments).
