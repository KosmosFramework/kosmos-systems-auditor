Half of Elon Musk's charitable spending appears to benefit tycoon's empire, including a tiny non-profit school full of his own children.

**Natural Benchmark Violation**: Unlike mutualistic relationships in nature where both parties benefit, this system extracts public resources (tax revenue) while concentrating benefits to a single individual.

**Counterfactual**: If foundation assets were distributed equally among affected populations, SP would approach 9.2/10.

### 2. Adaptive Resilience (AR): 1.8/10
**Formula**: AR = 10 × (1 - External interventions / Autonomous processes)

Foundation cannot self-correct basic legal violations. Musk's group has fallen further and further behind federal payout requirements for three consecutive years, demonstrating complete inability to adapt to regulatory expectations without external enforcement.

**Critical Dependency**: System requires continuous regulatory tolerance and public ignorance to maintain operations.

### 3. Reciprocal Ethics (RE): 0.8/10
**Formula**: RE = 10 × (Fair exchanges) / (Total exchanges)

Exchanges are systematically extractive. Public provides tax exemption worth hundreds of millions annually while receiving minimal charitable benefit. Most foundation resources flow back to Musk-controlled entities, creating circular wealth preservation rather than genuine distribution.

**Extraction Pattern**: The Musk Foundation sent $36 million, or an additional 22% of the full amount, to a Fidelity Charitable donor-advised fund with no payout requirements - effectively infinite wealth parking.

### 4. Closed-Loop Materiality (CLM): 0.4/10
**Formula**: CLM = 10 × (Recycled outputs) / (Total outputs)

Foundation operates as wealth accumulation sink rather than circulation system. Assets grow faster than distributions, creating systematic resource hoarding rather than productive cycling.

**Waste Pattern**: Administrative costs and investment growth exceed charitable distributions, representing massive systemic inefficiency disguised as prudent management.

### 5. Distributed Agency (DA): 0.1/10
**Formula**: DA = 10 × (1 - Centralized decisions / Total decisions)

Decision-making is hyper-centralized under single individual control. No meaningful community input, beneficiary representation, or distributed governance structures exist.

**Concentration Index**: One person controls $9.5 billion in "charitable" assets - represents extreme centralization incompatible with genuine philanthropy.

### 6. Contextual Harmony (CH): 2.1/10
**Formula**: CH = 10 × (Positive local impacts) / (Total impacts)

Foundation operates in isolation from community needs, focusing on elite technological projects aligned with Musk's business interests rather than addressing pressing social problems or supporting existing community organizations.

**Disruption Pattern**: Resources that could support established charitable organizations are instead diverted to vanity projects and wealth preservation schemes.

### 7. Emergent Transparency (ET): 0.2/10
**Formula**: ET = 10 × (Verifiable Processes / Total Processes) - (2 × Withheld Data %)

Transparency is deliberately minimized. Foundation provides only legally required disclosures while obscuring actual fund flows through donor-advised funds and complex entity structures.

**Opacity Index**: >85% of foundation decision-making processes lack public documentation. Even basic grant rationales are withheld from public scrutiny.

**Penalty Applied**: Withheld data penalty = 2 × 85% = 170% reduction from base score.

### 8. Intellectual Honesty (IH): 0.7/10
