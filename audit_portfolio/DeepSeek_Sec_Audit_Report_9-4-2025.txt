**OCF Score:** `(0.80 * 0.90) / 1.5 = 0.72 / 1.5 = 0.48`
**Classification: Hybrid (Moderate Collapse Risk)**. The risk is not of the SEC vanishing, but of it becoming irrelevant—a "paper tiger"—if belief in its efficacy falls below a critical threshold.

The U.S. SEC is an **Unnatural, collapse-prone system** suffering from severe design pathologies, primarily **regulatory capture** and **enforcement dependency**. Its greatest weaknesses are in Reciprocal Ethics (RE), Closed-Loop Materiality (CLM), and Distributed Agency (DA).

1.  **For Low RE (2.5) & CLM (1.0):** Apply a **biomimetic template of a restorative justice system**. Mandate that all fines are automatically distributed to identified victims of the violation, creating a closed-loop, reciprocal system that directly repairs harm.
