## Adversarial Readings

### Counterfactual 1: "Apple's Environmental Leadership is Genuine"
**Challenge:** "Apple has reduced emissions 60% since 2015 and leads industry sustainability."
**Response:** Cherry-picked metrics conceal fundamental contradictions. While operational emissions decreased, total product lifecycle emissions increased due to higher sales volumes (224.7M iPhones vs. 169M in 2015). Environmental improvements serve as marketing cover for accelerated material throughput.

### Counterfactual 2: "Carbon Offsets Provide Legitimate Climate Benefit"
**Challenge:** "Apple's forest projects in Kenya and China sequester real carbon."
**Response:** Class action lawsuit evidence suggests non-additionality - these forests were likely protected regardless of Apple's payments. Even if legitimate, offsets don't address the fundamental extraction model requiring continuous raw material depletion for planned obsolescence cycles.

### Counterfactual 3: "Technological Innovation Solves Environmental Problems"
**Challenge:** "Recycling robots and efficiency improvements prove genuine environmental commitment."
**Response:** Technical innovations improve the efficiency of an inherently unsustainable system. The Daisy robot processes ~240 phones/hour while Apple sells ~25,700 iPhones/hour globally. Innovation theater cannot overcome mathematical impossibility of infinite growth on finite planet.
